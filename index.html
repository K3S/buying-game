How it works

Buy one product for a year. Beat the computer.

You place orders by hand, month by month, trying to keep customers served while spending the least. Sounds simple for a single item — that’s exactly the point.

1

Order once a month

Each month you choose how much to buy. What you order takes a 2-month lead time to arrive — order now, it lands two months later.

2

Demand is hidden

You commit your order before you see that month’s demand. And demand will spike and dip without warning — just like real life.

3

Two costs to balance

Order cost ($2 each time you buy) versus carrying cost (20%/yr of value for everything sitting in stock). Order too often, or hold too much — either way you pay.

4

Hit 95% service

Keep enough on hand to fill 95% of demand. Run short and it’s a stockout (backorders allowed, but they hurt your score).

5

A free head start

You begin with stock that covers the first 2 months — those don’t count. Your score is the 12 months after that (Month 3–14).

6

You vs. the computer

At the end, the computer plays the exact same demand you faced. Lowest total cost that still hits 95% wins.

Takes about 2 minutes.
