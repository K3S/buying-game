How it works

Buy one product for a year. Beat the computer.

You place orders by hand, month by month, trying to keep customers served while spending the least. Sounds simple for a single item — that’s exactly the point.

1

Order once a month

Each month you choose how much to buy. What you order takes a 2-month lead time to arrive — order now, it lands two months later.

2

Demand is hidden

You commit your order before you see that month’s demand. And demand will spike and dip without warning — just like real life.

3

Every sale earns money

Each unit of demand you can fill sells at the retail price and earns revenue. A stockout isn’t just a miss — it’s a lost sale, and lost sales are lost profit.

4

Two costs eat into it

Order cost (a flat fee each time you buy) and carrying cost (20%/yr of value for stock just sitting there). Order too often, or hold too much — either way it comes out of profit.

5

Still hit 95% service

You must fill 95% of demand to qualify. Miss it and you can’t win, no matter how much you saved — chronic stockouts lose customers.

6

A free head start

You begin with stock covering the first 2 months — those don’t count. You’re scored on the 12 months after (Month 3–14).

7

You vs. the computer

The computer then plays the exact same demand. Whoever clears 95% service with the most profit (revenue − costs) wins.

Takes about 2 minutes.
